As discussed in lecture, basic algebra operations actually break down in floating point arithmetic. Take for instance the associativity property of the real numbers under addition where the following would be true,

$$ x + (y + z) = (x + y) + z \qquad x, y, z, \in \mathbb{R} $$

But in floating point arithmetic, the equality relation does not necessarily hold.

Find an $x, y, z$ such that, $x + (y + z) \neq (x + y) + z$. Enter your numbers below.

Hint: think about the concept of machine epsilon.
