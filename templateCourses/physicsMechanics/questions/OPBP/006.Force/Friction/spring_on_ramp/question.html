In the figure below m = {{ params.m }} kg, $\theta$ = {{ params.theta }}$^\circ$, and k = {{ params.k }} N/m. In this problem assume that the ramp never moves and that there is friction between the block and the ramp.
Part 1
If the coefficient of static friction between the block and the ramp is $\mu_s$ = {{ params.us }}, what is the maximum amount that the spring can be stretched beyond its equilibrium length before the block begins to slide up the ramp?
Part 2
Suppose now that the block is sliding up the ramp at a constant velocity. By what length is the spring stretched? Assume that the coefficient of kinetic friction is $\mu_k$ = {{ params.uk }}.
